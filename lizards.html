Lizards

Lizards is the most successful group of all reptiles (almost 6000 species). They are making up about 60% of all reptilian species, and can be found everywhere in the world, except Arctic and Antarctic. Lizards typically have limbs and external ears. They also have 4 legs: some are large and powerful e.g. Iguana, while others are short and almost redundant.

Watch Those Eyes

  • Able to tell a lizard from a snake by looking at its eyes
  • Most lizards have eyelids that can be opened and closed
  • Snakes do not have eyelids
  • Exceptions - some lizards have a transparent scale that covers the eyes and others do not have eyelids at all

Legless Lizards

  • Some lizards have no legs
  • Adaptation to ‘swim’ their way through loose soil and burrow underground
  • Legless lizards often mistaken as snakes

Defence mechanism

As lizards are a source of food for many animals, they have developed many interesting ways to try and scare intruders away.

Change color
  • Skin of chameleon has several layers of color cells
  • Beneath these layers is another type of cell with tentacle-like arms (melanophores)
  • Color change caused by melanophores moving the color cells in and out of the upper layers of skin
Drop tail (Autotomy)
  • On tail bones of lizards are certain weak points called fracture planes
  • Tail breaks off at these weak points
  • Muscles are formed in such a way that they come apart easily
  • Blood vessels are constricted at the weak points, so do not bleed when tails disconnect
  • Replacement tail begins to grow immediately, but new tail will look different from original tail
  • This defense strategy is well developed in geckos, skinks, wall lizards, racerunners, whiptails and glass lizards
  • Biting, bashing, scratching - done as a last resort
Appear larger

Some lizards will puff out their throats to appear larger and more menacing, such as the bearded dragon, while others will inflate their bodies. The horned toad lizard inflates its body to the point that it appears like a spiked balloon.

Make surprise

The element of surprise is another method of defense. The blue-tongued skink sticks out its blue tongue and scares predators away by making them think it's poisonous.
