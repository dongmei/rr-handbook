Reptile Enrichment

Enrichment for the reptiles in the Singapore Zoo comprises of the different categories such as food, social, enclosure design and housing, training programs.

Food enrichment

Food enrichment is conducted through chaning the below factors for the delivery of food:

  • Frequency
  • Schedule
  • Presentation
  • Processing of food items

Food items can also be presented in different ways such as strung up, hidden, scattered and in puzzle feeders.

The puzzle feeders consist of both naturalistic and artificial looking enrichment devices. It encourage exploration and investigative behaviour in the reptiles and will require the animals’ manipulation in order to get to the food

Social enrichment

Social enrichment includes displaying animals of the same species or different species in an exhibit. The Sungei Buaya where the Estuarine crocodiles, the Snake-head fish and the Giant Malayan terrapin are displayed together is an example of a mixed species exhibit

Example of human interaction as social enrichment would be public feedings and encounter sessions.

Enclosure design and housing

The enclosure design should provide complexity, visual barriers and substrates. Housing should include light, temperature, ventilation, humidity to match that of the species natural microhabitat, water management, enclosure furniture. Outdoor enclosures should have adequate sunlight and shade.

Training programs

Komodo dragons undergo training to facilitate husbandry and veterinary procedures. Keepers train them to enter a raceway at the back of their enclosure voluntarily so that they are able to conduct close up inspection on the animals

The estuarine crocodiles are also trained to respond to a cue (usually tapping of the water surface with a pole) to facilitate shifting. Once they respond to the cue, a piece of meat will be given to them as a positive reinforcer. This allows the keepers to safely shift the animals individually to specific locations at the Sungei Buaya exhibit.
